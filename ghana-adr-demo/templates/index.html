ADR Reporting Protocols

Ghana FDA pharmacovigilance reporting guidelines

Step-by-Step Reporting Workflow

1

Detect

Identify suspected adverse drug reaction using clinical criteria. Look for temporal association between drug administration and onset of symptoms.

2

Document

Record patient details (age, sex, weight), drug name, dose, route, start/stop dates, concomitant medications, and full ADR description.

3

Assess Causality

Evaluate causality using the WHO-UMC scale: Certain / Probable / Possible / Unlikely. Consider dechallenge and rechallenge outcomes.

4

Report (REQUIRED)

Submit Yellow Card to Ghana FDA within 15 days for non-serious ADRs. 7 days for serious or unexpected reactions. Use the online portal or paper form.

5

Follow Up

Monitor patient outcome and update the initial report with resolution status, treatment given, and any long-term sequelae.

6

Feedback Loop

Review Ghana FDA signal assessment communications and update prescribing practices. Attend quarterly pharmacovigilance review meetings.

SERIOUS ADR CRITERIA — IMMEDIATE REPORTING REQUIRED

  • ►Fatal or life-threatening reaction
  • ►Requires hospitalisation or prolonged inpatient care
  • ►Results in persistent or significant disability
  • ►Congenital anomaly or birth defect
  • ►Medically significant event (anaphylaxis, severe skin reaction, organ toxicity)
